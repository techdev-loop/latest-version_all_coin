Polymarket 15-Minute Cryptocurrency Trading Bot
I want to develop a commercial-grade automated trading bot that trades the 15-minute cryptocurrency bull/bear market (24/7) on Polymarket. The bot should continuously open positions on both bull/bear (YES) and bear (NO) markets during the 15-minute period, ensuring an average trading cost of less than $1 for YES+NO pairs. This should generate stable profits even after deducting fees and slippage.

Bot Key Features

Continuously monitors the currently active 15-minute cryptocurrency market (e.g., 15-minute BTC bull/bear market, ideally extending to ETH/SOL).

Executes orders on both bull/bear (YES) and bear (NO) markets during each 15-minute market period.

Reduce the average transaction cost of hedged exposures to less than $1 (with a settable target, e.g., $0.96 to $0.99) using an average bid/discount bid (DCA) strategy.

Manage inventory to ensure that risk exposures are roughly hedged, or implement explicit hedging logic in case of uneven execution.

Reliable Execution: Reliably handle partial executions, failed orders, sudden spread fluctuations, and order close times.

Key Success Criteria: Generate net profit after accounting for fees and realistic slippage, and consistently demonstrate an average fee of less than $1 per YES+NO currency pair in a live operating environment.

Profitability Requirements (Acceptance Criteria)

We are not interested in bots that simply trade. Profitability must be demonstrated under realistic conditions.

Please provide backtest or replay data using historical 15-minute chart data (or, if possible, sophisticated simulations using order book snapshots).

Provide mock trading and small-scale real-time pilot mode and document the following:

Average entry cost (YES+NO) for each hedged currency pair

Actual cost compared to estimated commissions/slippage

Net profit/loss by day and market timeframe

The bot must not trade if it cannot meet its cost threshold (no forced executions with costs exceeding $1.00).

(While future profits cannot be guaranteed, the implementation must demonstrate clear evidence of its ability to achieve the average cost target of less than $1.00 and demonstrate positive results in testing.)
